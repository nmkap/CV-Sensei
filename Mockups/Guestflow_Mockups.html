CV Sensei — Guest Flow

Option A: Try before you sign up · Supplement to main wireframe set

When a first-time visitor clicks "Get started free", they go directly into the product — no account required. They complete a full coaching session as a guest. The sign-up gate appears only when they click "Download PDF", at the moment of maximum motivation. By then, they've already experienced the product's value and have a CV they want to keep.

Portfolio case study note

This guest flow is worth documenting as a deliberate design decision in your case study. The key UX principle at play is progressive commitment — asking for personal information only when the user has already experienced value, and has something concrete to lose (their CV session) by not signing up. Compare this to "sign up first" flows where conversion happens before value is demonstrated. The gate framing — "Your CV is ready" vs "Sign up to continue" — is a specific copywriting decision with measurable UX impact, and worth calling out explicitly.
