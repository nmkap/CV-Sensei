Design Principles Applied
◐
60–30–10 Rule
60% neutral (dark + surface backgrounds), 30% supporting (mid tone for text, borders, secondary UI), 10% accent (burnt orange for CTAs, active states, highlights). Your old palette had ~50% in the orange family — breaking this rule.
Now: Dark 60% · Mid 30% · Accent 10%
▤
Tonal Hierarchy
A palette should create clear visual steps — Dark → Mid → Light — so depth, layering and information hierarchy can be expressed through tone alone. Your original dark (#222831) jumped straight to light (#EEE) with nothing in between. #6B6560 fills this gap.
Dark 0.02 → Mid 0.16 → Surface 0.87 luminance
◎
Purposeful Accent
An accent colour works because of its rarity and contrast with neutrals. Having two near-identical oranges (1.65:1 between them) dilutes impact. The refined approach keeps one accent identity (#B55400) but contextualises it — lighter on dark backgrounds as a technical necessity, not a new colour.
One accent identity. Two context-aware values.
♨
Colour Temperature Harmony
The dark (#222831) has a cool blue-grey undertone. The original #EEEEEE and #FFFFFF are both cool and sterile, creating tension with the warm burnt orange accent. #F2F0EC (warm off-white) bridges this — it has orange undertones that make the accent feel at home.
Cool dark · Neutral mid · Warm accent · Warm surface
